In memoriam of all the house rules that we used to follow because of our cat. Such as, to not leave the windows open at any time, keep delicate objects well of reach from him. Rules I don not follow anymore.

Here lies the many school lunches that I ate extremely fast so that I was not left behind, eating alone in the canteen. Soon I realized, I needed to find better friends.

Below are buried the collection of Christmas gift cards, I personally made for each of my school friends. This tradition was stopped after moving away for university.

In loving memory of all the mithai (sweet) shops that me and my grandfather discovered together for the first time, on our Kolkata trip.

Buried are the collection of planets, quests and stars from my Mario Galaxy game. After extensive afternoons playing with my brother, we reached the highest level. I hold pride in this collection we built together.

Deeply buried are the tools and skills developed to pass the Mario Galaxy games.

Rest in peace to the imaginary games I played with my brother. All the characters invented with only our hands and our imagination will live in my heart.

In remembrance of the many names of potential lovers I met through Tinder. They gave me entertainment, confidence and helped in my own self-development. Some left behind friendships.

Resting beneath this hallowed ground lies all the local amusement parks in my hometown that I can no longer visit, as they are not operational or are torn down to make way for new developments.

In loving memory of the numerous football player names, that I memorized because of my ex-boyfriend, who held the sport very dear to his heart.
